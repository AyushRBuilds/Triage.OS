Hyperkalemia is an elevated level of potassium in the blood. Normal potassium levels are between 3.5 and 5.0 mEq/L. Levels above 5.5 mEq/L are considered hyperkalemic, and above 6.5 mEq/L are life-threatening.
Signs include muscle weakness, palpitations, and ECG changes (peaked T waves).
Treatment includes calcium gluconate for membrane stabilization, insulin with dextrose, and potentially dialysis.
