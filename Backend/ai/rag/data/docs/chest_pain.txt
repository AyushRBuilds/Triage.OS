Chest pain in the emergency department requires immediate assessment for Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS), pulmonary embolism, and aortic dissection.
Initial workup includes an ECG within 10 minutes of arrival, troponin levels, and a chest X-ray.
High-risk patients (ST-elevation, hemodynamic instability) should be prioritized for catheterization or thrombolysis.
Symptoms of ACS include substernal pressure, radiation to the left arm or jaw, and diaphoresis.
